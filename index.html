Itinerary

Day 1: Oct 1 => Antananarivo - Arrival from Nosy Be - Leave for Andasibe (Night walk visit)

Upon landing at Ivato International Airport, you'll be warmly greeted by our representative who will assist you with your luggage and guide you to your comfortable 4x4 vehicle. Begin your journey eastward, traversing scenic routes towards Andasibe. The drive offers picturesque views of Madagascar's diverse landscapes, from bustling urban areas to tranquil rural scenes.

Upon arrival in Andasibe, prepare for an exciting night walk visit. Equipped with flashlights, you'll venture into the forest with a knowledgeable guide to spot nocturnal wildlife. Keep an eye out for lemurs, chameleons, frogs, and other creatures that come alive after dark.

Overnight : Andasibe Cyperus Hotel ★★★

Day 2: Oct 2 => Visit Analamazaotra National Park + Vakona Reserve

In the morning, embark on an exploration of Analamazaotra Reserve, home to the largest lemur in Madagascar, the Indri. Alongside the Indri, you may encounter other lemur species such as the common brown lemur and the eastern woolly lemur. The reserve also hosts a variety of birds, reptiles like the Parson's chameleon and the tiny ground chameleon, and other wildlife.

In the afternoon, visit the Vakona Reserve, where you'll have the opportunity to see the black and white ruffed lemur, the eastern bamboo lemur, the ring-tailed lemur, the brown lemur, and the diademed sifaka. The reserve's diverse inhabitants and lush surroundings make it a perfect spot for wildlife enthusiasts.

Overnight : Andasibe Cyperus Hotel ★★★

Day 3: Oct 3 => Roadtrip from Andasibe to Antsirabe (Stop at Ambatolampy pottery + Antsirabe city tour)

Embark on a scenic road trip from Andasibe to Antsirabe, with a notable stop at Ambatolampy to witness traditional aluminum pot casting. This fascinating process offers insight into local craftsmanship and culture. Upon reaching Antsirabe, often referred to as "the city of waters," you will explore its charming surroundings, including various workshops showcasing semi-precious and precious stones. The city tour provides a glimpse into Antsirabe's unique blend of natural beauty and artisanal heritage.

Overnight : Royal Palace Hotel ★★★

Day 4: Oct 4 => Antsirabe - Morondava (Early departure to watch the sunset in the Baobab Alley in Morondava)

The road trip from Antsirabe to Morondava is a picturesque journey that offers a glimpse into Madagascar's diverse landscapes and cultural heritage. Start early in Antsirabe, a city known for its thermal springs and vibrant markets. As you head towards Morondava, you'll pass through scenic highlands and local villages, providing a unique opportunity to witness the daily life of Malagasy people.

Along the way, make stops at notable spots such as:

  • Baobab Alley: Arrive in Morondava to witness the iconic Baobab Alley at sunset. The sight of the ancient baobabs silhouetted against the setting sun is a breathtaking experience.

This road trip not only provides scenic beauty but also cultural enrichment, making it a memorable part of your Madagascar adventure.

Overnight : Vezo Beach Hotel ★★★

Day 5: Oct 5 => Morondava - Kirindy Reserve (Visit the Kirindy forest by day and night)

Embark on an immersive journey from Morondava to the Kirindy Reserve, renowned for its rich biodiversity. During the daytime, explore the forest's winding trails to spot unique flora and fauna, including the iconic fosa and various lemur species. As night falls, the forest transforms, offering a chance to witness its nocturnal residents. Equipped with flashlights, venture into the darkness with a knowledgeable guide to encounter night-active creatures, making for an unforgettable wilderness experience.

Overnight : Relais du Kirindy Hotel ★★★

Day 6: Oct 6 => Kirindy (visit Sacred Baobab + In love Baobab then return to Morondava)

Begin your day with a visit to the Sacred Baobab, a tree revered for its age and cultural significance. Then, head to the In Love Baobab, known for its two intertwined trunks symbolizing eternal love. Capture the unique beauty of these magnificent trees before returning to Morondava.

Overnight : Vezo Beach Hotel ★★★

Day 7: Oct 7 => Morondava - Antsirabe (Tritriva + visit to Lake Andraikiba)

The journey from Morondava to Antsirabe takes you through some of Madagascar's most picturesque landscapes. As you leave behind the coastal beauty of Morondava, you'll travel inland towards the highlands, where you will visit Lake Tritriva, a breathtaking crater lake surrounded by stunning natural scenery.

Continue your adventure with a visit to Lake Andraikiba, known for its serene waters and rich local legends. The journey is a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural insights, providing a unique glimpse into the heart of Madagascar.

Travel from Antsirabe to Antananarivo, passing through picturesque landscapes. This journey offers scenic views of Madagascar's highlands and rural life, providing a glimpse into the daily activities and natural beauty of the region.

Overnight : Chalet des Roses Hotel ★★★

Day 9: Oct 9 => City tour of Tana - souvenir shopping - visit to the Queen's palace then transfer to the airport.

Begin your final day in Madagascar with a comprehensive city tour of Antananarivo, the vibrant capital city. Explore its bustling markets, colonial architecture, and historical landmarks. Engage in souvenir shopping to collect unique mementos and gifts. Visit the Queen's Palace, a significant cultural and historical site, to deepen your understanding of Madagascar's heritage. Conclude your journey with a transfer to the airport for your departure, carrying cherished memories of your adventure.

Money, credit cards and expenses

Understanding Money in Madagascar:

  • Currency: The Malagasy currency is the Ariary (MGA), divided into 5 Iraimbilanja.
  • Availability: Ariary is primarily available at local banks, official exchange offices in hotels, and the airport in Antananarivo.
  • Recommendation: We suggest bringing cash in Euros (EUR), British Pounds (GBP), or US Dollars (USD) for exchange. Tip: Exchanging a small amount (200-350 EUR) for Ariary upon arrival at the airport is convenient. You can exchange more throughout your trip as needed. Remember, leftover Ariary cannot be converted back to foreign currency, so exchange smaller amounts at a time. Important: Avoid black markets for currency exchange.

Credit Cards:

  • Limited Use: Credit cards are only accepted in some hotels, primarily in major cities like the capital (Antananarivo) and provincial capitals.
  • Fees Apply: Using your credit card may incur fees depending on your bank.
  • ATMs: Withdrawing cash from ATMs is possible at major banks, but ATMs are not widely available, especially in rural areas.

Bottom Line: Cash is king in Madagascar, especially outside major cities. Plan to use cash for most purchases.

Visa Requirements

For stays in Madagascar, a short-term visa is required. Here's a breakdown of the cost:

  • Less than 15 days: €10 (administrative fee)
  • More than 15 days: €35 (visa fee)

Obtaining Your Visa:

There are two convenient ways to secure your visa:

  1. Apply Online: This is the recommended option to avoid any potential delays upon arrival. Visit the official government website:  https://evisamada-mg.com/en/home
  1. Visa on Arrival: Obtain your visa upon arrival at the airport immigration office by presenting your passport.

Tips and gratuities

We believe tipping is a personal choice for each traveler. However, it's important to understand that tipping is customary in Madagascar, where wages tend to be lower. Consider tipping to show your appreciation for good service.

Here's a general guideline:

  • Drivers: €5 USD per day
  • Local Guides: €3-€5 USD per day
  • National Guides: $8-$10 USD per day
  • Restaurant Waiters: 10% of the bill (adjust based on service quality)

Explanation:

  • Drivers: Drivers typically receive a smaller tip compared to guides.
  • Local Guides: Local guides, who might be specific to a town or region, can be tipped in this range.
  • National Guides: National guides, who are often more experienced and have broader knowledge, can receive a slightly higher tip.
